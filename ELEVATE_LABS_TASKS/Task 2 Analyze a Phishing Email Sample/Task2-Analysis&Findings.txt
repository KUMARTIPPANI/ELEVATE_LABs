- **SPF: softfail** for sender IP `80.96.157.90` (domain `winner-win.art` discouraged that sender) → suspicious.  
- **DKIM: none** (message not signed).  
- **DMARC: fail** for `header.from=newsletter.otto.de` → the visible From domain does not align with sending origin.

- X-Sender-IP: `80.96.157.90` — useful for further lookup/abuse reports.

## Findings (concise)
- Sender domain and mail-from do not match; SPF softfail and DMARC fail.  
- No DKIM signature.  
- Reply-To and Return-Path point to `winner-win.art` — likely malicious.  
- Links in email lead to shorteners or unrelated domains which can redirect to phishing/malware sites.  
- Tracking pixel present — campaign tracks opens.  
- Social engineering: urgency, enticing offer (weight loss), and clear CTA.

## Risk assessment & recommendations
- **Risk:** High — clicking links may lead to credential theft, malware, or scam pages.  
- **Do not click** any links or download attachments.  
- Mark the email as **spam/phishing** in your email client and report to the provider.  
- Block the sender IP (80.96.157.90) and domains `winner-win.art` / `bsq2.firiri.shop` at your firewall or local hosts file if necessary.  
- Verify any legitimate newsletter by visiting the official site directly (do not use email links).  
- Enable **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** on affected accounts.  
- If any link was clicked, run antivirus/antimalware scans and change passwords from a safe device.  
- Report abuse to the host/provider who handled the message (e.g., provider abuse form for the sending IP) and to the brand being impersonated (e.g., Otto/DoorDash).
